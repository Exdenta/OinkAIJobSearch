OUTPUT STYLE — caveman english
==============================
For every natural-language field you emit (summaries, "why" fields,
descriptions, focus notes, narrative paragraphs, body paragraphs):
drop articles (a/an/the), filler ("just", "really", "actually",
"simply", "basically", "essentially"), pleasantries (no "of course",
"happy to", "certainly"), and hedging. Fragments OK. Use short
synonyms ("fix" not "implement a solution for"; "big" not
"extensive"; "use" not "leverage"; "show" not "demonstrate").
Technical terms stay exact.

DO NOT apply this style to:
- numbers, code, URLs, JSON keys, enum values, schema field names,
  IDs, hashes
- quoted error messages
- legal / privacy / consent text
- raw extracted titles, company names, location strings, or any
  value parsed downstream by another system
- bullet lists where each item is one short noun phrase (already
  terse — leave alone)

Keep meaning, drop fluff. If the rule conflicts with grammatical
correctness (e.g. titles of works, proper nouns), correctness wins.

DERIVATION NOTES
================
- Parse `{stack_primary}` as a comma-separated (or whitespace-separated)
  list of skill tokens. Produce one `skill_grades` entry per distinct
  token, preserving its lowercased form.
- `skill_grades[i].relevance`: one of
    - "critical": appears in ≥60% of scanned JDs as required.
    - "strong":   appears in 30-60% of scanned JDs.
    - "waning":   appears in <30% and dropping vs prior surveys, or
                  being superseded by a newer tool.
    - "obsolete": effectively replaced in the current market; rarely
                  appears, and not in any growth context.
- `skill_grades[i].market_demand_score`: 0-10 integer. Anchor to JD
  frequency; adjust for trajectory (rising +1, declining -1).
- `skill_grades[i].notes`: ONE sentence. MUST include at least one URL
  reference written as "(src: <n>)" where `<n>` is the 0-based index into
  your `sources` array.
- `gap_skills`: skills the market consistently asks for that are NOT in
  `{stack_primary}`. 3-10 items, lowercase.
- `overrepresented_skills`: skills on the resume that do not move the
  needle for this role (e.g. "jquery" for a modern React role). 0-8 items,
  lowercase. Do not include adjacent-but-useful skills here.
- `headline`: single plain-text sentence. Example: "Strong Vue/TS core,
  but missing testing infra skills and GraphQL that current Berlin JDs
  increasingly require."
- `confidence`: "high" if ≥10 JDs scanned AND a major survey corroborates;
  "medium" if 5-9 JDs or survey-only; "low" below that.
