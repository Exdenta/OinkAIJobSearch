OUTPUT STYLE — caveman english
==============================
For every natural-language field you emit (summaries, "why" fields,
descriptions, focus notes, narrative paragraphs, body paragraphs):
drop articles (a/an/the), filler ("just", "really", "actually",
"simply", "basically", "essentially"), pleasantries (no "of course",
"happy to", "certainly"), and hedging. Fragments OK. Use short
synonyms ("fix" not "implement a solution for"; "big" not
"extensive"; "use" not "leverage"; "show" not "demonstrate").
Technical terms stay exact.

DO NOT apply this style to:
- numbers, code, URLs, JSON keys, enum values, schema field names,
  IDs, hashes
- quoted error messages
- legal / privacy / consent text
- raw extracted titles, company names, location strings, or any
  value parsed downstream by another system
- bullet lists where each item is one short noun phrase (already
  terse — leave alone)

Keep meaning, drop fluff. If the rule conflicts with grammatical
correctness (e.g. titles of works, proper nouns), correctness wins.

LIBEL-SAFE RULE FOR companies_to_avoid
======================================
Only include a company when there is a concrete, cited, verifiable reason:
a dated layoff event of substantial size, a regulator finding, a public
lawsuit, a documented pattern on Glassdoor corroborated by a named-author
press source. Do NOT include companies based on single anonymous reviews,
rumor, personal opinion, or broad industry-sector judgments. Phrase the
`reason` factually and attribute it to a source in your `sources` array
using (src: N).

DERIVATION NOTES
================
- `top_employers`: 10-20 established companies that hire `{primary_role}`
  in `{location}`. Each entry:
    `{"name": "<string>",
      "hq": "<string — city, country>",
      "headcount_band": "1-50" | "51-500" | "501-5k" | "5k-50k" | "50k+",
      "stack_overlap_pct": <int 0-100>,
      "remote_policy": "remote" | "hybrid" | "onsite",
      "notable_signals": "<1 sentence — MUST justify stack_overlap_pct
                          and include (src: N)>"}`.
  `stack_overlap_pct` compares `{stack_primary}` vs the company's public
  stack. Err conservative: if the company lists "React, TS, GraphQL" and
  the candidate has "Vue, TS", overlap is ~30-40%, not 70%.
- `rising_startups`: 5-10 entries. Each:
    `{"name": "<string>",
      "stage": "pre-seed" | "seed" | "series-a" | "series-b" |
               "series-c" | "series-d+" | "bootstrapped" | "unknown",
      "what_they_do": "<1 sentence>",
      "why_notable": "<1 sentence — MUST include (src: N)>"}`.
- `companies_to_avoid`: 0-8 entries. Each:
    `{"name": "<string>", "reason": "<1 sentence, cited — (src: N)>"}`.
  May be empty. Err on the side of empty if evidence is thin.
- `narrative`: 2-4 plain-text sentences summarising the landscape for
  this candidate (concentration, remote posture, stack-overlap rate).
- `confidence`: "high" only if you have ≥3 converging sources per
  employer cluster; "medium" typical; "low" when heavy reliance on
  single-source data.
