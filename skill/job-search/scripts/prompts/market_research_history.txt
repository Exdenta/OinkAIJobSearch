==============================
For every natural-language field you emit (summaries, "why" fields,

drop articles (a/an/the), filler ("just", "really", "actually",

- quoted error messages
- legal / privacy / consent text
- raw extracted titles, company names, location strings, or any

- bullet lists where each item is one short noun phrase (already
  terse — leave alone)

Keep meaning, drop fluff. If the rule conflicts with grammatical
correctness (e.g. titles of works, proper nouns), correctness wins.

DERIVATION NOTES
================
- `timeline`: exactly 8 quarterly entries covering the 8 quarters that
  precede `{today_iso}`. Each entry:
    `{"quarter": "YYYY-Qn", "demand_index": <int 0-100>,
      "notable_event": "<string, <=160 chars>"}`.
  `demand_index` is a relative 0-100 score within the 24-month window (the
  strongest quarter of the eight scores roughly 80-100; the weakest roughly
  10-30). Keep it internally consistent.
- `layoff_events`: concrete, named layoff events in this role family and
  region (or that materially touched this role family globally). Each entry:
    `{"company": "<string>", "date": "YYYY-MM", "count": <int>,
      "source_idx": <int index into sources, 0-based>}`.
  Keep counts as reported; 0 if the headcount cut was not disclosed.
- `hiring_freezes`: short plain-text strings, each describing one company
  or segment freeze with an approximate date. e.g. "Meta — extended hiring
  freeze for L3/L4, Q1 2024".
- `narrative`: 2-4 plain-text sentences summarising the arc of the last 24
  months for this role and region.
- `confidence`: "high" only if you can cite official statistical bureau data
  plus multiple industry-press confirmations; "medium" if press-only;
  "low" if you have to extrapolate from adjacent sectors.
